BLOOD 34+34
erythrocytes · plasma · types
  • List 3 major plasma proteins & roles?
    Albumin (osmotic pressure, transport), Globulins (immunity, transport), Fibrinogen (clotting).
  • What is hematocrit? normal range?
    Hematocrit = % RBC in blood. Male ~42-52%, female ~37-47%.
  • Explain biconcave advantage of RBC?
    Increases surface/volume ratio for gas exchange; allows deformation through capillaries.
  • How does Hb bind O₂ & CO₂ simultaneously?
    O₂ binds to heme iron; CO₂ binds to globin chains (carbaminohemoglobin).
  • Difference between plasma and serum?
    Plasma contains clotting factors; serum is plasma minus fibrinogen & other clotting proteins.
  • Blood types: antigens of A, B, AB, O?
    A: A antigen; B: B antigen; AB: both A & B; O: none.
  • Rh incompatibility mechanism?
    Rh- mother exposed to Rh+ fetal blood produces anti-Rh IgG that crosses placenta and destroys fetal RBCs.
  • Most abundant leukocyte?
    Neutrophil (50-70%).
  • Normal platelet count?
    150,000 – 400,000/μL.
  • What is polycythemia?
    Excess RBCs (increased hematocrit).

HEART 33+33
chambers · valves · conduction
  • Position & size of heart?
    In mediastinum, size of fist, apex left, base superior.
  • Layers of pericardium?
    Fibrous parietal, serous parietal (pericardial sac), visceral (epicardium).
  • Tricuspid valve location?
    Between right atrium and right ventricle.
  • Chordae tendineae function?
    Anchor AV valves to papillary muscles, prevent prolapse.
  • Conduction system order?
    SA node → AV node → bundle of His → bundle branches → Purkinje fibers.
Which structure contains Purkinje fibers?
A) SA node B) AV bundle C) ventricular subendocardium D) atrial myocardium
✅ C) ventricular subendocardium

    Small vessels that supply large vessel walls.
  • Femoral artery continuation?
    External iliac artery becomes femoral after inguinal ligament.
